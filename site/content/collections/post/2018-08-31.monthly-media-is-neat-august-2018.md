<p>Oh boy did August fly by! In around a move and deadlines I still found time to watch and play and read some stuff. Awaaaaaaaaay we go!</p>
<p><strong>Film:</strong></p>
<figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-width="1600" data-orig-height="2400"><img src="https://78.media.tumblr.com/c9e54b8b6ff8aea3de788a52d4c35a76/tumblr_inline_pe8rooWirA1qdydna_500.jpg" alt="image" width="500" height="750" data-orig-width="1600" data-orig-height="2400" /></figure>
<p><b>Eighth Grade </b>(2018)<br />
I haven’t felt as stressed as I did during this movie in a long time. While a completely different generation, it’s interesting to see what parts of growing up are consistent. Oh and the soundtrack was great! It was just such a weird and enjoyable movie.</p>
<p><b>Television:</b></p>
<figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-width="750" data-orig-height="649"><img src="https://78.media.tumblr.com/a285251f1aae43fe81a23ccfcbedd603/tumblr_inline_pe8rxw6iVX1qdydna_500.jpg" alt="image" width="500" height="433" data-orig-width="750" data-orig-height="649" /></figure>
<p><b>The Bachelorette </b>(Episode 14.10 to 14.11)<br />
It’s a bummer that Becca kinda swept the stuff about Garrett under the rug and that they barely addressed any of it. Missed opportunity. Also Blake is way too thoughtful to be the lead of this insane show.</p>
<p><b>Bachelor in Paradise</b><br />
I was reeeeeeeally resistant to get into this season but after just half a dozen episodes, I’m back! I’m all for this crazy mingling and we get way more time with the cast just hanging out. There’s still way too much emphasis on the generic lines during the dates (who isn’t looking for someone honest and kind?) but outside of that, it’s all good good dating drama.</p>
<p><b>The Great British Baking Show </b>(Episode 2.04 to 2.05)<br />
Slow burn. It’s a nice, recharging, break from the Bachelor world. This season has been great and next week is sweet dough!</p>
<p><b>Reading:</b></p>
<figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-width="1000" data-orig-height="737"><img src="https://78.media.tumblr.com/5ed32545abe73b206a2148204123fd82/tumblr_inline_pe8s4qmrtn1qdydna_500.jpg" alt="image" width="500" height="369" data-orig-width="1000" data-orig-height="737" /></figure>
<p><b>After the End of the World </b>by Jonathan L Howard<br />
(Page 68 of 355)<br />
Already a fantastic sequel (the first book, <i>Carter &amp; Lovecraft</i> is all the psychological horror of its namesake, with 1/100th of the xenophobia). So it’s about a bookstore owner and detective up to typical PI antics but set in an alternate reality where Nazis are commonplace (more commonplace than the real world…oof).</p>
<p><b>Bachelor Nation: Inside the World of America’s Favorite Guilty Pleasure </b>by Amy Kaufman<br />
(Complete)<br />
Perfect companion to my favourite reality tv franchise. It perfectly balances the peeks behind the curtain with the social commentary on the show. It also touches on a little psychology and sociology and it all uses the show as a framing device. It may be the end of the summer but defo pick this up for your next beach read.</p>
<figure class="tmblr-full" data-orig-width="965" data-orig-height="700"><img src="https://78.media.tumblr.com/61f8bd4538b3618228354534aae92d9b/tumblr_inline_pe8s9tjW0r1qdydna_500.jpg" alt="image" width="500" height="363" data-orig-width="965" data-orig-height="700" /></figure>
<p><b>Head Lopper, Vol. 2: Head Lopper And The Crimson Tower  </b>by Andrew MacLean<br />
(Complete)<br />
The world is getting bigger and the mysteries continue to grow. I really dig this series and while there’s a clear shift in artistic style that happens halfway through the arc, I’m still on board. Head Lopper is like Indiana Jones or Mad Max in that he doesn’t really change, but his presence affects those around him. It’s still a wonderfully pulpy read and I can’t recommend it enough!</p>
<p><b>Black Hole </b>by Charles Burns<br />
(Complete)<br />
The premise is simple enough but it’s really not about teens who mutate in weird and sometimes gross ways after they have sex; it’s so much more. The art reflects the simplified outlooks on life that many of the characters present, and the whole thing is just something that sticks with you, you know? Now I get why it’s on all those “must read” lists.</p>

<p><b>Fear of a Black Dragon  </b>(Podcast)<br />
Great podcast that reviews different D&amp;D modules. There are classic adventures, independent modern adventures, and everything in between! Episodes aren’t super frequent (they mostly review modules they’ve run, so that takes time) but each one is amazing. Check it out.</p>

<p><b></b><b>Dungeons &amp; Dragons Hexcrawl Campaign </b>(Wizards of the Coast)<br />
The summer has been light for this campaign so all that’s happened since last month was a return to society and the discovery that a temple housing a necromantic tome has been destroyed (so probably not a big deal).</p>
<p><b>Horror At Havel’s Cross </b>(Winghorn Press)<br />
Continuing on with this bite-sized D&amp;D adventure, the party has done a little sleuthing at a crime scene and fought a bunch of ghouls at a temple of death! I really can’t recommend this adventure enough. It’s great for new players (and new dungeon masters too!) and has all the hits when it comes to a good adventure: fights, mystery, and exploration!</p>

<p><b>Minit </b>(Devolver Digital)<br />
Your character dies after 60 seconds and yet it never feels repetitive. Using time as a puzzle element makes this an almost 4-dimensional game. It’s cute and engaging and beautiful and most importantly, fun!</p>
